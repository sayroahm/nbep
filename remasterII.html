Our Mandate

Mission, Objectives, and Organizational Values

NBEP operates as a sophisticated bridge between open-source digital intelligence and law enforcement intervention, functioning strictly within legal boundaries and through established reporting channels.

Core Mission Statement

The National Bureau of Exploitation Protection exists to reduce the prevalence of child sexual exploitation on the internet through systematic identification, documentation, and reporting. We believe that informed civilian participation — conducted ethically and transparently — can meaningfully augment the capacity of law enforcement agencies tasked with combating this crisis.

NBEP does not engage in vigilante justice, direct confrontation, entrapment, or any activity that would compromise the legal integrity of a law enforcement investigation. Our role is precisely defined: intelligence gathering and formal reporting to recognized authorities.

The Scope of the Problem

Digital exploitation networks operate across a spectrum of platforms, from indexed social media to encrypted private channels. The volume of illicit content generated and distributed daily far exceeds the investigative bandwidth of any single agency. NBEP supplements official capacity by maintaining a trained volunteer network that monitors, documents, and escalates identified threats through proper legal channels.

Our volunteers understand the psychological burden of this work and receive ongoing support and structured operational protocols designed to minimize exposure while maximizing the quality and utility of the intelligence packages transmitted to law enforcement.

Relationship to Law Enforcement

NBEP maintains no formal governmental status and claims no law enforcement authority. We do not make arrests, issue warrants, or conduct confrontations. All intelligence collected by NBEP operatives is transmitted securely to the Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI), the Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3), the National Center for Missing and Exploited Children (NCMEC), and relevant local agencies for independent verification and prosecution. NBEP functions as a reporting conduit, not an investigative arm of any government entity.

Our operational standards are designed to ensure that every intelligence package we deliver meets the evidentiary threshold required by federal investigators to initiate or augment an active investigation. We prioritize quality over quantity — a single well-documented report is of far greater value than dozens of incomplete or ambiguous submissions.

Organizational Ethics

Every NBEP volunteer is bound by a formal code of conduct that prohibits unauthorized access to computer systems, entrapment of individuals, fabrication or manipulation of evidence, unsanctioned communication with suspects, and any activity outside of the monitoring and reporting mandate. Violations of this code result in immediate removal from the coalition and, where applicable, referral to law enforcement.

Operational Boundary

NBEP volunteers observe and document. We do not interfere, confront, or engage suspects directly. All actions are limited to reporting through official channels.

Evidence Standards

All documented findings are metadata-tagged, timestamped, and preserved in accordance with federal digital evidence guidelines to ensure legal admissibility.

Important Disclaimer

NBEP is not a law enforcement agency and has no governmental authority. We do not guarantee prosecution outcomes. All reports are submitted to, and acted upon solely by, certified law enforcement agencies.

Confidentiality

The identity of all reporting parties and NBEP volunteers is held in strict confidence. No personal information is shared without explicit consent or legal compulsion.

Operational Capabilities

How NBEP Identifies and Responds to Digital Exploitation

Our structured operational workflow ensures that every identified threat is handled with precision, documented to legal standards, and escalated to the appropriate federal or local authorities without delay.

01

Open-Source Intelligence Monitoring

Volunteer analysts conduct systematic monitoring of public and semi-public digital environments using legal open-source intelligence (OSINT) methodologies. This includes social media platforms, public forums, file-sharing networks, and known distribution channels associated with exploitation material.

All monitoring is conducted using lawfully accessible information only. No unauthorized system access is permitted under NBEP operational protocols.

02

Evidence Documentation and Preservation

When potential exploitation material or suspect conduct is identified, NBEP analysts follow a strict evidence preservation protocol. All documentation is timestamped, hash-verified, and stored in a secure chain of custody to meet federal digital forensic standards. This includes URL capture, metadata archiving, and contextual documentation.

NBEP does not download, store, or transmit exploitative content. Documentation is limited to metadata, links, and contextual information for law enforcement referral.

03

Federal Intelligence Transmission

Completed intelligence packages are securely transmitted to appropriate federal and local agencies including the FBI, IC3, and NCMEC. Each submission includes full contextual documentation, timestamped evidence logs, relevant identifiers, and an analyst summary prepared according to FBI submission standards.

Submissions are reviewed internally before transmittal to ensure completeness and accuracy. Incomplete or unverified reports are not forwarded.

04

Threat Network Mapping

Beyond individual incident reports, NBEP analysts work to identify patterns of activity that suggest organized exploitation networks. Relational data mapping assists in identifying systemic actors rather than isolated instances, enabling law enforcement to pursue higher-impact prosecution targets.

Network mapping is conducted using only lawfully accessible data and connection analysis from open sources.

05

Public Education and Awareness

NBEP contributes to public awareness efforts around digital child safety, online grooming patterns, and the proper channels available to parents, educators, and community members who suspect exploitation. We provide guidance on how to recognize warning signs and engage with official reporting systems directly.

Educational content is reviewed by qualified professionals and updated regularly to reflect the evolving digital landscape.

06

Volunteer Training and Operational Readiness

All NBEP volunteers undergo a structured onboarding process covering legal frameworks, ethical operational boundaries, psychological preparedness, evidence documentation protocols, and secure communication practices. Ongoing training ensures operational standards are maintained as the digital landscape evolves.

Volunteers are never exposed to actual exploitative content as part of their training or operational duties. NBEP strictly prohibits any such exposure.

Formal Reporting Protocol

Reporting Pathways and Agency Coordination

NBEP's reporting architecture is designed to ensure every submission reaches the appropriate federal or local authority with the level of documentation required to initiate actionable investigation.

1
Initial Identification and Triage

A trained NBEP analyst identifies suspect content or conduct through lawful open-source monitoring. The finding is logged with initial metadata and assigned to a case file. A preliminary severity assessment is conducted to determine the appropriate reporting pathway and urgency level.

2
Evidence Documentation and Verification

The assigned analyst completes full documentation in accordance with NBEP's forensic standards protocol. This includes URL logging, metadata extraction, hash verification where applicable, and a written contextual summary. A senior analyst reviews the documentation for completeness and accuracy prior to case advancement.

3
Internal Case Review

Completed cases are reviewed by NBEP's internal review committee to assess documentation quality, confirm legal compliance of the surveillance methodology, and approve the case for transmittal to the appropriate agency. Cases that do not meet minimum documentation standards are returned for supplementation.

4
Secure Transmittal to Authorities

Approved intelligence packages are transmitted securely to the designated agency — FBI, IC3, NCMEC, or local law enforcement — using each agency's official submission channels and protocols. A transmittal reference number is logged for tracking purposes.

5
Case Archive and Follow-Up

All transmitted cases are archived securely in the NBEP case management system. Where publicly available information indicates that a submitted report contributed to a prosecution, this is noted for coalition records. NBEP does not engage in post-submission follow-up with law enforcement agencies.

Regulatory and Legal Context

Legal Framework Governing NBEP Operations

NBEP operations are conducted in strict accordance with applicable federal and state laws. The following provides an overview of the primary legal frameworks that define and constrain our activities.

PROTECT Our Children Act

Federal law establishes mandatory reporting obligations for organizations that become aware of child exploitation material. NBEP's reporting protocol is structured to fulfill and exceed these obligations by routing all findings to the appropriate federal agencies through designated channels, including the NCMEC CyberTipline as required by statute.

NBEP volunteers are trained to understand their individual reporting obligations under this act and organizational reporting procedures ensure compliance in all cases.

Relevant Authority: PROTECT Our Children Act of 2008, 18 U.S.C. § 2258A

Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA) Compliance

All NBEP monitoring activities are confined strictly to lawfully accessible information and systems. NBEP prohibits any form of unauthorized access to computer systems, networks, accounts, or devices. Volunteers are instructed that any activity that would constitute unauthorized access under the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act is absolutely prohibited and constitutes grounds for immediate removal and potential referral to law enforcement.

Relevant Authority: Computer Fraud and Abuse Act, 18 U.S.C. § 1030

Electronic Communications Privacy Act

NBEP monitors only publicly accessible digital communications and does not intercept private electronic communications. Volunteer activities are explicitly prohibited from involving the monitoring of private messages, encrypted communications, or any digital communication for which there is a reasonable expectation of privacy, in compliance with federal wiretapping statutes.

Relevant Authority: Electronic Communications Privacy Act, 18 U.S.C. § 2510 et seq.

Non-Governmental Status and Limitations

NBEP is a private, non-governmental organization with no law enforcement authority, no power of arrest, and no governmental mandate. NBEP does not represent any government agency and makes no claim to governmental authority. All information provided on this website is for informational purposes only. NBEP's reporting activities do not constitute legal advice and do not guarantee law enforcement response or prosecution outcomes.

Organizational Integrity

Operational Standards and Ethical Commitments

NBEP maintains a rigorous code of operational ethics to ensure that our activities remain lawful, effective, and above reproach. These standards are non-negotiable and uniformly applied across all volunteer roles.

Legal Compliance

All activities are conducted within the boundaries of applicable federal and state law. No unauthorized access, entrapment, or extralegal action is permitted under any circumstances.

Evidence Integrity

Documentation standards ensure all evidence is unmodified, properly attributed, and preserved in a manner consistent with federal digital forensic requirements for admissibility.

Volunteer Welfare

NBEP recognizes the psychological toll of this work. Mandatory wellness check-ins, structured operational limits on exposure, and access to mental health resources are core components of our volunteer program.

Confidentiality

Volunteer identities, reporting party information, and active case details are held in strict confidence. Disclosure is made only where required by law or with the explicit consent of the individual concerned.

Join the Coalition

Volunteer with the National Bureau of Exploitation Protection

NBEP is sustained entirely by the commitment of volunteer specialists from across the country. We are not a government-funded entity and do not compensate volunteers monetarily. We offer the opportunity to contribute meaningfully to the protection of children through disciplined, legally grounded work.

Volunteers are the operational backbone of NBEP. Our coalition includes individuals with backgrounds in digital forensics, cybersecurity, criminal justice, legal research, data analysis, and community advocacy. Regardless of your professional background, if you possess relevant skills and an unwavering commitment to ethical conduct, we invite you to consider joining our coalition.

Participation in NBEP requires a formal onboarding process, including a background review, orientation to our operational standards and legal framework, and completion of our internal training curriculum. This process exists to protect our volunteers, ensure the integrity of our work, and maintain the trust of the law enforcement agencies we serve.

Available Volunteer Roles

  • Digital Intelligence Analyst — Open-source monitoring, threat identification, and intelligence documentation in accordance with NBEP forensic protocols.
  • Case Documentation Specialist — Evidence preservation, metadata logging, and case file preparation for federal transmittal.
  • Legal Research Volunteer — Ongoing research into applicable statutes, case law, and regulatory developments relevant to NBEP operations.
  • Public Education Contributor — Development and distribution of educational resources for parents, educators, and community stakeholders.
  • Training and Onboarding Coordinator — Development and delivery of internal training materials and new volunteer orientation programs.
  • Technical Infrastructure Volunteer — Maintenance and security of NBEP's internal systems, secure communications architecture, and case management platform.
