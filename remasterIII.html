Mission Directive

The National Bureau of Exploitation Protection operates as a non-governmental intelligence gathering entity. Our primary objective is the systemic disruption of sexual exploitation networks. Unlike government agencies that are often slowed by bureaucratic layers, our vigilante collective moves with speed to identify predators and extract digital evidence.

We believe that digital sovereignty comes with a responsibility to protect those who cannot protect themselves. Our team focuses on high-level target acquisition, mapping hidden network nodes, and verifying content legitimacy before engaging federal partners.

How We Operationalize Intelligence

Stage 1: Identification & Tracking
NBEP volunteers utilize sophisticated OSINT tools to locate exploitation material across cleared and onion networks. We identify the source and the movement of illegal content.
Stage 2: Metadata Verification
Once identified, our tech specialists verify the metadata and geolocation markers of the evidence to ensure it meets the strict evidentiary standards of federal prosecutors.
Stage 3: Law Enforcement Handover
Verified packets are forwarded to the IC3, FBI Child Exploitation task forces, and local police chiefs. We provide a clear trail for them to execute warrants.

Volunteer Notice
